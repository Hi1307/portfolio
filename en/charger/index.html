How to use the charger

  1. Check if the charger is hot to the touch
    • If the charger is unbearably hot immediately, UNPLUG ALL DEVICES immediately.
  2. Check the ports available on the charger, and the recommended devices that can be charged. The reference is given below. Use your own cables.
  3. Unplug devices once completed.

Power output for each port

Total for the entire charger: 65W

  • Port USB-C1

    • If used alone: Up to 65W, at 20V/3.25A. Requires a 3.25A cable. Up to 45W (3A cable) at 15V/3A and 30W (2A cable) at 15V/2A.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Apple MacBook®, MacBook Air®, and MacBook Pro®
      • Other large 13" and 15" laptops
      • Nintendo Switch
      • Android phones that support charging >60W (DO NOT PLUG IN IPHONES)
    • If 2 or 3 ports are used on the charger: Up to 40W at 20V/2A. Port C1 and C2/A are connected in parallel, and the current splits, but the voltage remains the same.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Apple MacBook®, MacBook Air®, and MacBook Pro®
      • Other large 13" and 15" laptops
      • Nintendo Switch
      • Android phones that support charging >40W (DO NOT PLUG IN IPHONES)

  • Port USB-C2

    • If used alone: Up to 65W, at 20V/3.25A. Requires a 3.25A cable. Up to 45W (3A cable) at 15V/3A and 30W (2A cable) at 15V/2A.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Apple MacBook®, MacBook Air®, and MacBook Pro®
      • Other large 13" and 15" laptops
      • Nintendo Switch
      • Android phones that support charging >60W (DO NOT PLUG IN IPHONES)
    • If USB-C1 and USB-C2 are used together: Up to 22.5W at 20V/1.125A.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Android® Phones
      • iPhones®
      • Nintendo Switch
      • MacBooks will charge, but very slowly
    • If USB-C1 and USB-A are used together/all 3 ports are used: Up to 11.25W at 10V/1.125A. USB-C1 and USB-A are connected in series, and the voltage splits.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Android® Phones
      • iPhones®
      • Nintendo Switch
      • Use USB-C1 to charge MacBooks and laptops

  • Port USB-A

    • If USB-C2 isn't used: Up to 22.5W at 20V/1.125A.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Android® Phones
      • iPhones®
      • Nintendo Switch
      • MacBooks will charge, but very slowly
    • If USB-C2 is used: Up to 11.25W at 10V/1.125A. USB-C1 and USB-A are connected in series, and the voltage splits.
    • Recommended devices:
      • Android® Phones
      • iPhones®
      • Nintendo Switch
      • Use USB-C1 to charge MacBooks and laptops
